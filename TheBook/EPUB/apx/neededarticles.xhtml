Needed Articles

Order of personal importance to me:

  1. Mammalian Dentition

Mammalian Dentition

This is desperately needed.

Dentition is frequently used in mammal descriptions and is often a reliable diagnostic for distinguishing similar species or genera. Many of the articles assume the reader has both a basic understanding of mammalian dentition and the terminology used to describe mammalian dentition. Many people however do not.

Describing the terminology is, in my opinion, beyond the scope of the glossary. Definitions should be in the glossary, but there should also be an article that does a better job that the glossary can refer to.

This is, in my opinion, a desperate accessibility need that is needed to help non-biologists get the full value of the journal articles.

The article should be written such that a ‘typical’ high school student can comprehend it.

The article should be written as a stand-alone article that can be reused as needed.

Colorado Wood Rat

Volume 1 Number 5 has an excellent article detailing observations about a wood rat in Colorado that created a home within an abandoned building. I think it would be really fascinating for a naturalist in Colorado who stumbles upon this type of observation to make a modern account of the observation with detailed digital photographs to match the account.
