Donations

I am a strong supporter of the Open Access concepts presented by Aaron Swartz (may he never be forgotten) in his Guerilla Open Access Manifesto, including:

“Only those blinded by greed would refuse to let a friend make a copy. Large corporations, of course, are blinded by greed.”

For some people, even charging $1.00 results in a financial access barrier that prevents access to the resource. Those people are not any less worthy than Jeff Bezos or Mark Zuckerberg. This is why this digital book is provided for free without any charge.

That being said, an incredible amount of time, energy, and even some personal financial resources went into the production of this digital book.

In 1920, $3.50 would have given you a 1 year subscription to the Journal of Mammalogy. In 2020 assuming I did my maths right, that would be the equivalent of about $45.00.

I only ask you for $10.00, split between Pipfrosch Press and the American Society of Mammalogists. You can donate more to either if you would like, of course, or donate less or even donate none. The choice is completely yours to make and depends upon both your financial means and your personal level of generosity.
