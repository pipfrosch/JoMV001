Many glossary definitions defined here are sourced from Wiktionary.org. If the definition from Wiktionary.org is added to, the date of Wiktionary.org access is appended to the end of the definition.

A. O. U.
Abbreviation for American Ornithologists’ Union—which now goes by the name American Ornithological Society.
basal
1. Basic, elementary; relating to, or forming, the base, or point of origin. 2. (anatomy) Associated with the base of an organism or structure. Wiktionary 2020 April 16
binomial nomenclature
The scientific system of naming each species of organism with a Latinized name in two parts; the first is the genus, and is written with an initial capital letter; the second is some specific epithet that distinguishes the species within the genus. By convention, the whole name is typeset in italics [or is underlined when italics can not be used]. The genus part is often abbreviated to its initial letter e.g. H. sapiens for Homo sapiens. Wiktionary 2020 April 16
calcar
A spur of cartilage that runs from the inner ankle along the outer interfemoral membrane.
cladistics
A commonly used term to refer to phylogenetic systematics.
crepuscular
Active at or around dusk, dawn or twilight. Wiktionary 2020 April 26
distal
Remote from the point of attachment or origin. [Contrast with basal]. Wiktionary 2020 April 16
DOI
An abbreviation for Digital Object Identifier—A persistent unique identifier for a digital object that can be used to resolve the canonical version of a digital object, such as an academic paper.
estivate
To go into stasis or torpor in the summer months. Wiktionary 2020 April 18
fibula
The smaller of the two bones in the lower leg, the calf bone. Wiktionary 2020 April 16
IBIS
Abbreviation of Indian Biodiversity Information System—A ‘a series of web-based, modular and searchable biodiversity portals, designed to compile and present in a comprehensive and scientifically accurate manner the available information on Indian flora and fauna which is readily accessible, free and user-friendly.’ See https://www.indianbiodiversity.org/.
ITIS
Abbreviation of Integrated Taxonomic Information System—A database of taxonomic names including reports on the current consensus regarding the validity of those names. The stated goal of the ITIS “is to create an easily accessible database with reliable information on species names and their hierarchical classification.”
junior synonym
When a taxonomy name is assigned to a type specimen but the type specimen is not actually distinguishable as a distinct taxon from a previously described type specimen, the more recent taxonomy name is considered to be a junior synonym of the previously described taxonomy name and in all but a few very special circumstances—such as the senior name violating a naming convention—the junior synonym is considered invalid.
metatarsus
The part of the foot between the toes and the ankle, especially its five bones. Wiktionary 2020 April 16
nom. nov.
An abbreviation for Nomen Novum—A renaming of a taxon that previously had been assigned a taxonomical name but for which the former name must be invalidated.
nomen nudem
‘Naked Name’—When a new taxonomic name is given but without a type specimen or an adequate description of the type, is considered invalid and referred to as nomen nudem.
patagium
The thin membrane that extends between the limbs and body of a bat or of gliding mammals. Wiktionary 2020 April 16
phylogenetic systematics
Phylogenetic systematics is the formal name for the field within biology that reconstructs evolutionary history and studies the patterns of relationships among organisms. See https://evolution.berkeley.edu/evolibrary/article/phylogenetics_01.
sp. nov.
An abbreviation for Species Nova—A new species that has not previously been assigned a taxonomy binomial nomenclature name.
tarsus
The part of the foot between the tibia and fibula and the metatarsus. Wiktionary 2020 April 16
tibia
The inner and usually the larger of the two bones of the leg or hind limb below the knee, the shinbone. Wiktionary 2020 April 16
tragus
The small piece of thick cartilage of the external ear that is immediately in front of the ear canal. Wiktionary 2020 April 16
type locality
The geographical locality where the specimen used to describe a new species was collected.
uropatagium
The portion of the patagium found between the hind limbs and connecting to the tail. Wiktionary 2020 April 16
vicissitude
A change from one thing to another, in the context of biology this can include taxonomy changes over time.
